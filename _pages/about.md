I am an integrative biologist with training in marine community ecology. My research investigates the causes and consequences of thermal trait variation within and among species to understand how shifting thermal regimes affects community assembly and ecosystem functioning. I draw from concepts in metabolic scaling, metacommunity theory, and trait-based community ecology, and integrate theory, experiments, and molecular approaches to address these aims. I am also passionate about social equity and human well-being in marine conservation, and conduct interdisciplinary research on this topic.
